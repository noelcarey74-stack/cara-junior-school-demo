Pupil Illness Policy

Policy on Pupil Illness in Cara Junior School

Deciding when to keep a child from school can be difficult, especially where a child may not be able to verbalise that they are unwell or in pain.

Some illnesses require absence from school to prevent infection spreading to other children or staff and to allow the child time to rest, recover and be treated.

If your child is not well enough to participate in normal school activities, the safest decision may be to keep them at home under observation.

Why this policy is important

Cara Junior School pupils may require a high level of support, supervision and routine during the school day. The school does not have nursing cover as part of the multi-disciplinary support team.

If a sick child is in class for the day, it may disrupt the educational activities of the class and may require significant staff attention. It may also increase the risk of infection spreading to other pupils and staff.

If a child becomes unwell during the day

If a child appears unwell during the school day, the school will contact parents or guardians where there are concerns about the child’s health or capacity to remain in school.

The school has a medical room where a child may rest while awaiting collection, but this is only an interim measure.

When to keep a child at home

Too Unwell for Activities

Keep the child at home if they are extremely tired, unusually irritable, crying without known cause, or not able to participate in normal activities.

Additional Care Needed

Keep the child at home if they need more care than staff can provide safely without affecting the health and safety of other pupils.

Restrictions Needed

If a child needs restrictions such as no PE, no yard or reduced activity, they may need to remain at home.

Infectious Illness

If a condition can be passed to other pupils or staff, the child should remain at home until the risk of infection has passed.

Poor Sleep or Exhaustion

If a child has not slept well or has been awake since early morning, consider keeping them at home under observation.

After Laxatives

If laxatives have been administered, the policy advises keeping the child at home for 24 hours.

Common illness and symptom guidance

The table below summarises the main guidance from the policy in a website-friendly way. Parents and guardians should seek medical advice where necessary.

Illness or Symptom Guidance
Antibiotics Remain at home until the child has no fever for 24 hours, indicating antibiotics are taking effect.
Asthma Absence is needed if the pupil has had an asthma attack and requires recovery time. The school should have a written health plan and medication authorisation.
Body rash with fever Seek medical advice. The pupil may return when a medical practitioner determines the illness is not communicable.
Chicken pox or shingles Remain at home until blisters have dried and crusted, usually 5–6 days after onset of rash.
Conjunctivitis May return 24 hours after treatment begins. If a GP decides not to treat, a note is required.
Severe cough, runny nose or mucus Medical attention may be necessary, especially with uncontrolled coughing, wheezing or difficulty breathing.
Diarrhoea Pupils and staff with gastrointestinal illness should stay at home until symptom-free for at least 48 hours.
Vomiting Parents will be contacted to collect the child immediately. Stay at home until symptom-free for at least 48 hours.
Fever with behaviour changes or illness Exclusion is necessary where fever is accompanied by behaviour changes or signs of illness.
Headlice Children may return after treatment and removal of live lice and nits from hair.
Impetigo / Staph / MRSA May return 24 hours after treatment starts. Wound must be covered with dressing taped on all four sides.
Ringworm May return after treatment begins. Area should be covered in school for the first 48 hours.
Scabies May return after treatment is started with a note from GP.
Strep throat May return after 24 hours of antibiotic treatment and no fever for 24 hours.
Vaccine preventable diseases Remain at home until judged not infectious by a medical practitioner.
Slapped cheek syndrome Medical advice may be needed. Keep child at home until rash has presented and they are no longer contagious.
This webpage is a clean summary of the policy. Parents and guardians should contact the school or seek medical advice if unsure whether a child should attend school.
