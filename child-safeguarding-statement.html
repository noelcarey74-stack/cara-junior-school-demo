Child Safeguarding Statement

Child Safeguarding Statement & Risk Assessment

Cara Junior School has adopted and follows child safeguarding procedures for schools. The school recognises that child protection and welfare considerations must be reflected in all school policies, procedures, practices and activities.

The Child Safeguarding Statement sets out how the school seeks to protect pupils from harm and how it manages child protection concerns in line with relevant school procedures.

Child safeguarding is everyone’s responsibility. All staff and school personnel are expected to act in the best interests of pupils and follow the school’s child protection procedures.

Purpose of this Statement

The purpose of the Child Safeguarding Statement is to provide a clear statement of the school’s approach to child safeguarding and to identify procedures that support the protection and welfare of pupils.

  • To promote a safe school environment for pupils.
  • To identify possible child safeguarding risks within school activities.
  • To record procedures in place to address and reduce those risks.
  • To support staff awareness of child protection responsibilities.
  • To ensure concerns are reported and managed appropriately.

Designated Liaison Person

The school’s Child Safeguarding Statement includes details of the Designated Liaison Person and Deputy Designated Liaison Person. These named roles should be confirmed against the current official school document before publication.

Before this page is made public, the school should confirm the current DLP, Deputy DLP, review dates and Board of Management approval dates.

Procedures in Place

The school’s safeguarding procedures include arrangements for reporting child protection concerns, maintaining records, training staff, managing recruitment, and ensuring school activities are supervised appropriately.

  • Child protection concerns are reported through the school’s child protection procedures.
  • Relevant staff training and awareness are maintained.
  • School personnel are made aware of child safeguarding responsibilities.
  • Child safeguarding records are managed carefully and appropriately.
  • School activities are planned with pupil safety and welfare in mind.

Risk Assessment Areas

The uploaded document includes a risk assessment table. The areas below present the main safeguarding risk themes in a clearer website format.

Harm Not Recognised or Reported

Procedures support staff in recognising and reporting child protection concerns appropriately.

Behaviour and Supervision

Supervision, positive behaviour supports and school procedures help reduce risks during the school day.

One-to-One or Small Group Work

Teaching, therapies, care supports and individual work should be managed with appropriate safeguarding awareness.

Intimate Care and Medical Needs

Intimate care and administration of medicine are supported by relevant school policies and careful record keeping.

Visitors and External Personnel

Visiting personnel, volunteers and external supports should follow school safeguarding procedures and supervision arrangements.

Online and Digital Safety

Internet use, images, recordings and digital communication should be managed in line with school policy and pupil safety requirements.

Policies Linked to Safeguarding

Child safeguarding connects with a number of other school policies and procedures. These may include behaviour, anti-bullying, intimate care, administration of medicine, internet acceptable use, data protection, health and safety, and school transport.

  • Code of Behaviour and Discipline Policy
  • Anti-Bullying Policy
  • Intimate Care Policy
  • Administration of Medicine Policy
  • Data Protection Policy
  • School Transport Policy
  • Health and Safety procedures
  • Acceptable Use / Online Safety procedures

Staff Awareness and Training

The school’s procedures include awareness of child safeguarding training and information. School personnel should be aware of the procedures to follow where a concern arises.

Review and Board of Management

The Child Safeguarding Statement and Risk Assessment should be reviewed by the school and the Board of Management in line with current requirements.

The uploaded document includes Board of Management sign-off and review areas. These should be checked and confirmed before the final live version is published.
