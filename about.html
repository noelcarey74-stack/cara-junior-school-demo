Our School

A caring learning environment

Cara Junior School caters for pupils aged 4–12 years with a dual diagnosis of autism and a mild or moderate intellectual disability.

The school opened in 2011 and is under the patronage of the Brothers of Charity. We are located in Mayfield, Cork City.

Cara Junior School provides a learning environment where each child can develop according to their individual needs. We support pupils through structured, positive and caring approaches across the school day.

Our Approach

Supporting communication, regulation and learning

The school uses a range of autism-specific approaches and supports to help pupils access learning and take part in school life.

Structured Learning

Structured teaching approaches support routine, independence and engagement.

Communication Supports

Supports such as PECS, communication boards, visual supports and Lámh may be used where appropriate.

Sensory Regulation

Pupils are supported in a calm and caring environment with attention to sensory needs and wellbeing.

Principal’s Welcome

A message from our Principal

It is a privilege to be part of the Cara Junior School community. Our school is built on care, respect, support and partnership with families.

Every member of staff is committed to helping each child feel safe, understood and valued. We work together to provide meaningful learning experiences and to celebrate the individuality of every pupil.

Parents and guardians are an important part of our school community, and we value strong communication between home and school.

Principal: Colette Butler
